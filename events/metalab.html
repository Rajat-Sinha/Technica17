Metalab

SIMULATE YOUR THOUGHT!!

About

MATLAB is a tool, one of its kind, in the hands of a metallurgist. It is used to simulate and solve complex problems in almost all fields of metallurgy with unerring precision. METALAB is the opportunity to solve, design and engineering problems in metallurgy using MATLAB.

Guidelines


PRELIMINARY ROUND
1. Team member - 1.
2. Elimination round.
3. 30 to 40 Objective type questions.
4. There will no negative marking.
5. Read the general instructions carefully written in the question paper.
6. Marks of each question will be indicated along with the question.
7. ‘*’ mark questions are important and carry more marks (as indicated in the sample question paper). In case of a tie between 2 or more candidate, the one who has corrected more ‘*’ mark questions will be promoted to next round.
8. Only 1 option will be correct in every question. If more than 1 option ticked then 0 marks will be considered.
9. Space for rough work will be given in the question paper itself.
10. Those who qualify preliminary round will be promoted to the next round.
11. The decision of the core committee will be final in case of any misunderstanding.

FINAL ROUND
1. Depending upon the number of students selected from preliminary round, several groups will be made by mixing up 1st, 2nd and 3rd year students.
2. The groups will be made by the event coordinator and core committee and the groups made will be final.
3. Each group will consist of 2 members selected randomly.
4. Bring Laptops for the final round of the MATALAB
5. 10 – 20 objective and 3 – 5 subjective questions will be asked.
6. Objective questions should be answered/ticked in the question paper itself.
7. Candidates should run subjective questions in MATLAB and submit the result/output/graph (as asked in the question paper) as a word file to the event coordinator.
8. The decision of the core committee will be final in case of any misunderstanding.
